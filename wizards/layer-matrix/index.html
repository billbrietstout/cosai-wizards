Work backwards from five accountable zones the CoSAI SRF separates on purpose—then see where mainstream IT/GRC catalogs still speak in generic terms. The left rail restates what each layer is for; each card names a risk family, where catalogs go thin, and a suggested control extension (borrowed wording from mature frameworks).

  1. L1 — Business & Usage. Who may use AI for what, under which risk acceptance, policy, and regulatory obligations—including lifecycle decisions such as approval, tiering, and retirement.
  2. L2 — Information. What data trains and feeds models, with what rights, lineage, and integrity—including corpora, embeddings, and retrieval—not only generic data-at-rest protection.
  3. L3 — Application. How AI capability is exposed through software: surfaces, APIs, tools, agents, prompts, and human override—behavior around the model.
  4. L4 — Platform. How inference runs as a shared service: tenancy, isolation, quotas, operational hardening, and abuse at runtime scale.
  5. L5 — Model provider. What the model artifact is, how it was produced, and how vendor or fine-tuned variants change over time—ML supply-chain accountability.
